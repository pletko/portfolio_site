Anime Tourism

In 2021, Netflix reported that half of its users chose to watch anime titles. For many anime fans, traveling to Japan and visiting locations inspired by or seen in their favorite anime is a dream come true, especially since Japan closed to foreign tourists amidst the 2020 pandemic.

This presents a huge opportunity for Anime Tourism, an online resource for anime fans to travel and experience Japanese culture.

Purpose: A conceptual responsive redesign of Anime Tourism 88 for CareerFoundry.

My Role: UX Researcher, UX/UI Designer

Timeline: 2 months

My Process

Research

User Interviews

User research began with user interviews involving 11 participants. While 5 participants are minimum recommended by NNgroup, I wanted extra practice with user interviews, especially since I had a larger group of interested partcipants.

User Personas

Three unique personas were created from the insights gained during user interviews.

User Flow

User flows were completed by "zooming out" in a big picture frame of mind, considering the user flow as a whole.

Ideation + Wireframes

After each major step in the design process, user feedback was integrated. Testing included paper prototyping, design critique, and preference testing.

Preference Testing

Preference Testing at the mid-fidelity level was conducted with UsabilityHub.

Design

A final look at the mobile screens.

Conclusion

Challenges:

When sizing up my largest screen, I was met with a lot of whitespace. While whitespace can be a powerful, my design felt empty. I resolved the issue by revisitng my MVP list and adding more features to the side bars of the Event Feed.

Future Iterations:

The next iteration would include a badge system. Users could earn and collect digital badges for real-life locations they visit.

Next Steps:

Based on user research, a great addition to this brand would be a native app with AR/VR capabilites, downloadable content, and on-the-go use.
