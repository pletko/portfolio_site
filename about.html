About

Former Tokyoite. Self-Proclaimed Nerd. Animation and Game Enthusiast. Coffee Addict. Animal Lover. Adventurer.

After graduating from Illinois Wesleyan University in 2019, I landed my first dream job: teaching English in Tokyo, Japan. I loved the two years I spent working with my Japanese, Chinese, Filipino, and Nepalese students who would utilize English as a main pillar in their careers. I also loved deep-diving into Japanese culture, traveling throughout the country, and dipping my toes into creative endeavors like the indie film industry, modeling, and travel writing.

However, I grew up being radically honest about my career indecision. Coming from small-town USA and being a first-generation college student, I knew I needed more time and resources to solve the problem of my chosen career path.

After exploring possibilities and passions, I realized I wanted a career where I could help people, be creative, tell stories, and continue to learn and grow. That's when I narrowed in on UX/UI.

To pursue this new career, I moved back to the US, settling in Chicago as a barista and puppy nanny in the interim.
